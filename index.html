Meta Ads Manager (blue)

Purchase and revenue data from Meta using a 7-day click + 1-day view attribution window. Meta counts a conversion if a customer clicked or viewed an ad within that window — even if they later converted via another channel. This tends to over-attribute revenue to Meta.

ClickMagick — Meta sources only (green)

ClickMagick tracks actual clicks and records the last paid click before purchase. CM columns show only purchases attributed to Meta placements (fb, ig, facebook, an, th). This is a more conservative, last-click view and will typically be lower than what Meta reports.

Blended ROAS

Total CM revenue from all sources divided by Meta ad spend. This matches what ClickMagick shows at the top of its dashboard. It reflects total business revenue generated while Meta was running — not revenue directly caused by Meta.

Why do Meta ROAS and CM ROAS differ?

Meta's 7-day window claims sales that happened days after the ad interaction — including sales driven by email, organic search, or direct revisits. CM only credits the last click. Together they give upper and lower bounds on Meta's actual contribution.
